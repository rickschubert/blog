Blood, Sweat, and Pixels - Jason Schreier - Book Review

I recently read Jason Schreier’s second book “Press Reset” and I liked it so much that I jumped to the opportunity of also reading his first book: Blood, Sweat, and Pixels - The Triumphant, Turbulent Stories Behind How Video Games Are Made. This book is much more hopeful than the first one and this is also why I ultimately enjoyed it a little bit less. It is a highly enjoyable read about a fascinating topic, but I do think that Schreier shines the most when he is being critical about its subject and pinpoints the problems of the industry.

Press Reset - Jason Schreier - Book Review

Press Reset by video game journalist Jason Schreier details how unstable development is with its always looming layoffs, office moves across the country and project cancellations. The book starts in the late 80s and early 90s with famous video game designer Warren Specter, detailing his move across various publishers, trying to make games he is passionate about but which never quite hit the nerve of a mass audience. From there on, the book moves forward through the decades, covering the experiences of dozens of industry figures.

I hate difficulty settings in video games

“Playing a video game on a lower difficulty is for losers. This is not how it was intended!” A sentence like the above races through my mind every time I contemplate lowering the difficulty - but rarely do I follow through with it. The voice in my head is too strong. Usually, I end up trying over and over again if there is a particularly difficult section in a video game - which in the end simply leaves me getting more and more frustrated and takes all enjoyment from playing.
