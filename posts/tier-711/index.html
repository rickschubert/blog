Tier 711

The date is 24 December 2027. Children like Tiff and Jill are wrapped up in their hermetically sealed onesies, eagerly awaiting Santa’s presents. What will he bring this year? A pony for Tiff? A magical dollhouse for Jill?

One floor further down, the adults are also wrapped up, but in protective hazmat gear. The glimmer of the TV reflects on Christie’s and Mike’s visors. “We are truly, outstandingly sorry, but under the current circumstances, we are unable to celebrate Christmas as planned. It was a difficult decision but one we believe is necessary: tomorrow, the region of London will move from tier 710 into the specific new tier 711.”

“711!” gasps Christie behind her helmet, causing her vision to steam up.

“In this specific new tier”, continues the prime minister, “children will be asked to vacate any living space they share with adults over the age of 23. For this purpose, our military forces have been working incredibly hard to construct housing solutions in the Docklands area.”

“This is horrible, Mike!”

“It is. But it’s for the best.”

“For the best? Those are our children!”

“They can kill us, honey!” Mike turns the TV off and gets up from the couch. His legs are sticking together from the polyester suit.

“That’s okay, I have lived my life already.”

“Christ, Christie, you just turned 26!”

“You might think it’s young, but this virus doesn’t.”

“It’s only for two weeks. Two weeks of isolation and then this will be over.”

“Of course it will. Of course!” says Christie with a sneer.

“This virus can’t live forever. If we restrain the hosts, it will die off.”

“This is so horrible.” Christie starts to cry. Mike is startled by this sight - he hasn’t seen his wife shed tears in more than a year, when the government announced a culling order for all dogs. Poor old Mr. Blinky.

Mike marches over to Christie and lets his glove sink on her shoulder. He can feel her bones through their combined layers of protection.

It turns out that Jill received her wish from Santa after all - both Tiff and Jill depart the next day to their new home for the upcoming weeks. “It’s a magical dollhouse, just like you wanted!” her mother has said. How exciting!
