I hate difficulty settings in video games

“Playing a video game on a lower difficulty is for losers. This is not how it was intended!”

A sentence like the above races through my mind every time I contemplate lowering the difficulty - but rarely do I follow through with it. The voice in my head is too strong. Usually, I end up trying over and over again if there is a particularly difficult section in a video game - which in the end simply leaves me getting more and more frustrated and takes all enjoyment from playing. Is this really what was intended?

To me, changing the video game difficulty away from the default is a psychological problem. I am painfully trying to create the experience the developers (supposedly) wanted me to have; in that way, I am vying for the author’s intent like high-school teachers are when discussing classic literature.

But how are choices on how difficult a game should be even made? Did the developers of the original Resident Evil have had their game tested for months on end by hundreds of players to find out how many items to hand out?

I have deep dismay for developers who don’t seem to understand that different people have different sets of skill - and different levels of frustration. A famous example of this is the game “Wolfenstein: The New Order” in which the game depicts the main character depending on the selected choice. When selecting the lowest difficulty, the main character is being shown with a baby’s bonnet and a dummy in their mouth.

A game like Wolfenstein is actively trying to stop the player from choosing this difficulty - but what would be the harm? It seems like many developers don’t understand that different people have different expectations of games: Some people want to feel like a mighty god. Some loathe repeating sections they have already seen. Some can’t spend that much time on gaming. Others may be handicapped in some way. Some are easily frustrated and angered.

Difficulty is such an essential element of game, almost the essence of gaming - yet the industry hasn’t made any major advancements in this area. Most video games still ask you at the start what difficulty to select - a choice no player can properly make as they haven’t even played a minute of the game yet! While most modern games nowadays allow for changes to the difficulty settings during a game session, there is still a big group of games which don’t allow the player to change the difficulty once they have started.

While most triple-A titles allow you to change the difficulty on the fly, they only allow you to go into one direction. Such an example is any game in the Uncharted series - you can only move from Normal to Easy, not from Easy to Normal. Other examples of this annoyance are the recent Resident Evil 2 and Resident Evil 3 remakes. The reason for only allowing the one-directional change in difficulty is usually that trophies are tied to certain difficulties. I say trophies should reward players who drain every last drop of game i.e. via meticulous exploration, not those who crave for a greater challenge.

Of course, there are a few outstanding exceptions to bland and restrictive difficulty choices, such as Resident Evil 4 which covertly readjusts the difficulty so that it’s ensured the player is always faced with a tense but fair challenge.

My mother used to say “Losers are only those who care too much about other people’s opinion”. A proverb which is absolutely right - I should lower the difficulty if I want to as it is my gaming experience. Thanks, Mom!
