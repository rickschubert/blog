Blood, Sweat, and Pixels - Jason Schreier - Book Review

I recently read Jason Schreier’s second book “Press Reset” and I liked it so much that I jumped to the opportunity of also reading his first book: Blood, Sweat, and Pixels - The Triumphant, Turbulent Stories Behind How Video Games Are Made.

This book is much more hopeful than the first one and this is also why I ultimately enjoyed it a little bit less. It is a highly enjoyable read about a fascinating topic, but I do think that Schreier shines the most when he is being critical about its subject and pinpoints the problems of the industry. While he is still doing that in his first entry, I have the feeling that he didn’t dare enough to stand by negative revelations. Although he often calls out crunch culture, layoffs and other problems, he finishes each chapter on a bright note, underlining what a great achievement it is that each video game finished production. I can’t speak for Schreier’s intentions, but one gets the feeling that he doesn’t want to taint the relationships with the industry figures he is interviewing and on whom he ultimately relies to create this book.

Nonetheless, this is another wonderful and fascinating read which I can highly recommend. Schreier manages to convey how difficult it is to make a video game and how young and disorganised the industry is. Upon reading this book one realises that it is a plain wonder that any video game even exists!
